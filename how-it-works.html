Why Casa

Built on trust — not fees.

Verified hosts

Every host is verified before listing. ID checked, property confirmed. You're talking to the person who actually owns the place — not a management company, not a rental agency.

Transparent pricing

No fees hidden until checkout. The nightly rate, any cleaning fee, and every charge is agreed between you and the host before any money moves. Nothing added by Casa.

Real reviews

Reviews are tied to confirmed stays. Guests can only review properties they've actually booked through Casa — so every 4.9 is earned, not gamed. Reviews also live publicly in the community feed.

Questions

Things people ask.

Is Casa really free for guests?
Yes — completely. Casa charges hosts a flat monthly fee to list their property. Guests pay nothing to Casa — not at search, not at checkout, not ever. The price you see is the price you pay the host directly.
How do I pay the host?
Directly. Once you enquire and the host confirms your dates, they'll send you their preferred payment method — bank transfer, PayPal, or similar. Casa is not involved in the payment. This keeps things simple and means no extra fees.
What if something goes wrong?
Because you're dealing directly with the host, any issues are resolved between you — the same way you'd sort things with a landlord or a hotel. Most hosts on Casa are experienced and responsive. Reviews are verified and public, so you can read real accounts before you book.
Is the property verified?
All hosts are ID-verified before their listing goes live. Property descriptions are submitted by the owner and are their responsibility — reviews from previous guests are the best way to verify the listing matches reality.
How much does it cost to list?
£19 per month, flat. No commission on bookings, no percentage of nightly rate, no hidden charges. Cancel any time. If you have more than one property, each property has its own listing fee.
Can I list if I'm already on Airbnb?
Yes — many hosts use Casa alongside other platforms. Some hosts migrate fully once they build up a direct booking base on Casa. Others use Casa as a way to fill gaps without paying Airbnb's commission on those nights.
What is the community feed?
The feed is a public forum where hosts and guests post. Hosts use it to announce availability. Guests post "looking for" requests. Both sides share reviews, local tips, and photos. Posts are organised by county and searchable by hashtag — it's designed to feel like a community, not a marketplace.
Is Casa only for the UK?
For now, yes — Casa is focused entirely on UK holiday rentals. England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland. Doing one thing well matters more to us than doing everything everywhere. International expansion may come later.

The Casa Promise

How Casa actually works, in plain terms.

No fine print, no "terms may vary." This is exactly how money moves, how cancellations work, and what a Verified badge actually means — the same rules whether you're booking your first stay or listing your tenth property.

01 — Why guests pay nothing

The price on the listing is the whole price.

Casa doesn't add a booking fee, a service charge, or any kind of checkout markup. There's no percentage taken from your payment, because Casa never touches your payment — see below. What the host lists is what you pay them, full stop.

That's possible because Casa doesn't make money from guests at all. It makes money from hosts, through a flat monthly listing fee — which means Casa's incentive is to help a host's property get seen and booked, not to squeeze a cut out of every night you stay.

02 — How hosts actually get paid

Directly. Casa is never in the payment.

Once a guest enquires through Casa and a host confirms the dates, the two of you arrange payment between yourselves — bank transfer is the most common route, though some hosts also take PayPal or another method they're comfortable with. The host tells you their preferred details directly in the conversation.

Casa isn't a payment processor and doesn't hold, escrow, or take a cut of that transfer. That's a deliberate choice, not a missing feature: it's what keeps the guest-side fee at genuinely £0, and it means a host gets paid on their own terms, at full value, without waiting on a platform payout schedule.

The trade-off is honesty about what that means: because Casa isn't in the payment, Casa can't reverse it either. That's exactly why reviews, message history, and host verification (below) carry real weight here — they're the trust layer that replaces a payment platform's buyer protection.

Hosts pay Casa separately — a flat £19 a month per listing, however many bookings that listing gets. No commission, no percentage of the nightly rate, ever.

03 — How cancellation actually works

Set by the host, agreed with the guest.

Every listing states a cancellation policy — most default to free cancellation up to 7 days before check-in, but hosts can set their own. You'll see the exact policy on the listing and again at checkout, before you commit to anything.

Because Casa doesn't process the payment, it also doesn't process refunds or enforce a cancellation policy on either side. If plans change, cancelling is a conversation directly between you and the host — the same as it would be with an independent cottage owner or a small B&B. Confirm the host's terms with them before you pay, and get any change in plan agreed in writing over Casa messages, so there's a clear record either way.

04 — What "Verified Host" actually checks

A badge that means something specific.

A green tick that means nothing is worse than no tick at all. On Casa, "Verified" and "Verified+" map to a fixed, published set of checks — never a vague trust signal.

Verified

All three required
  • Email address confirmed
  • Phone number confirmed
  • Government-issued photo ID confirmed against the account

Verified+

Everything in Verified, plus
  • A passed third-party background check — criminal record / right-to-let
  • Optional and host-initiated — never required to list or reach Verified

A host with no badge simply hasn't completed all three Verified checks yet — it isn't a penalty, and plenty of good hosts are mid-process. The badge you see on a host's profile and on every listing they're attached to reflects exactly the checks listed above, nothing implied beyond them — this is the whole standard, not a summary of a longer one.
